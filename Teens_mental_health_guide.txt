    <h2> <b>Do teens experience more emotions, and if so, why? </b></h2>

    <h3 class = "highlight"> Teens do experience critical changes because during this period; their brain is undergoing reorganization of the brain as certain parts of your brain mature faster than others. </h3> 
    
    <p>A study done by the National Library of Medicine found that the maximum grey matter was reached first by the sensorimotor cortex, while the prefrontal cortex matures last. This imbalance is why teens are prone to be more susceptible to emotions. This period is very important because it allows teens to grow into adults, as adolescence is where most of the development for their adult life occurs during this time. It is ok for you to be more susceptible to your emotions; that's perfectly normal and even a good thing. </p>

    <h2 class="p3"> <b>What is emotional intelligence, and what can I do to implement some strategies for it?</b></h2>
    
    <h3 class="highlight"><strong> Emotional intelligence is being in control of your emotions and also being able to  connect emotionally with others. </h3> </strong>
    
    <p>The most important parts of emotional intelligence are: </p>
    <div class = "container-list">
        <li> <p><strong>Recognize:</strong> being able to identify your emotions so that you can resolve the problem if the emotion is negative by finding the source of that emotion.</p> </li>
        <li> <p> <strong>Analyze:</strong> To be able to recognize your emotions, ask yourself “what am I feeling like right now” and “Why am I feeling [feeling] right now, and what caused it” </p> </li>
        <li> <p> <strong>Empathize:</strong> Developing empathy so that you can communicate with others effectively. The best way to get better at empathy is to ask more questions, listen more intently, and expose yourself to more people. </p>  </li>

<div class = "container"> 
    <h2> What is stress? </h2> 
    <h3 class = "highlight"> Stress is the body's response to changes and challenges. It is the body's way of responding to potential threats and also getting you ready to face what is coming ahead.</p> </li>
</h3>
</div>

<br>
<br>
<br> 

<div class = "container"> 
<h2>What causes stress to happen?</h2> 

   <h3 class = "highlight"> Stress triggers can vary from impending deadlines to test anxiety, and certain events cause you to stress because of certain factors that can cause us to worry. But we shouldn't worry about what events cause us stress; it's bound to occur. What matters is how we handle stress.</p> </li> 
</h3>
        
        
    </div>

<br>
<br> 

<div class = "container"> 

<h2 class = "p3"><strong>Stress Management stratagies</strong></h2>
   <p><strong>Identifying triggers</strong></p>
<h3 class = "highlight"> When you can figure out recurring events that stress you out can help you prepare for them. For example, when you know you will have a big test to take next week, you will feel stressed near the date of the test, so you can prepare by studying now to feel less stressed about your upcoming test. </h3> 
    
    <div class = "container-list"> 
    <li> <p> <strong>Plan:</strong>  When you have a lot of things to do, it can feel like you just don't have enough time to do it all, thus causing stress to ensue. One great strategy to deal with this stress is to schedule out when you can do things. Please note it doesn't have to be too rigid, just simply a guide to follow. </p> </li> 
    
    <li> <p> <strong>Mindfulness:</strong> Mindfulness is essentially the idea of responding to emotions rather than reacting to them. Mindfulness helps with processing emotions and also boosts focus and attention span.</p> </li>
    
    <li> <p> <strong> Mindful walks: </strong>  Take a walk in nature or outside without being on your phone and try to observe the things happening around you. </p> </li> 
    
    <li> <p> <strong> Meditation: </strong> Meditation can help you focus on your thoughts and relax your body. Here are the steps to it. First, find a comfortable place, you can lie down or sit up straight, and just pay attention to your breath and also your thoughts. Try to keep focus on your breath, and if you have thoughts during meditation (completely normal), just try to refocus back on the breath slowly. Take 10 to 20 minutes daily to do this. If you don't have that much time, just observe what is happening around you and be conscious of your breathing pattern; fast or slow, hard or soft, etc.</p> </li>

    <p> There are many ways to practice self-care for mental health, but here are a few key methods </p>
    
    <div class = "container-list">
        
    <li> <p> <b> Set clear intentions on what you want to do: </b> eg, saying 1 positive thing about yourself every day, doing one thing every day that makes you feel happy, or talking to a friend when you're sad </p></li>
    <li> <p> <b> Connect with others:</b> by trying to deepen connections with friends, you socialize more, which studies have shown that social connections help with enhancing mood and even decreasing mortality rates (NIH).</p></li>
    <li> <p> <b> Relaxation techniques:</b> Relaxing can help activate your parasympathetic nervous system, which is the part of the nervous system that allows for relaxation and reduces stress and body tension. Some strategies include deep breathing, visualizing a calm and soothing place, and progressively relaxing certain muscle groups at a time. </p> </li>
    <li> <p> <b> Turn emotions into actions: </b> when you feel a certain way, like sadness, take action to do something about it rather than suppress it. For example, you can listen to upbeat music or talk with a friend, just do something positive that makes you feel better. </p> </li>

<div class = "container">
    <h2>Four main types of negative thinking</h2>
    <div class = "container-list">
        
    <li> <p> <b>Personalizing:</b> blaming yourself for everything Solutiion:don't put all the blame on yourself, figure out what YOU specifically did wrong.</p></li>

    <li> <p> <b>Magnifying:</b> focusing only on the negative aspects of a situation with no regard to the positives: Solution:focus on what went well </p> </li>

    <li> <p> <b>Catastrophizing:</b> you expect the worst to happen without any logic to persuade you Solution:look at how you can handle the situation, and also apply logic to the situation.</p></li>

    <li> <p> <b> Polarizing: </b> you see the world in black and white or good and bad; you categorize situations without any middle ground. Find the opportunity that this situation brings and use it to shed light on the good aspects of the situation. </p> </li>
